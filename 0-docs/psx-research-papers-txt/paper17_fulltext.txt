Abstract 
This study examines the time series predictions of the KSE -100 index using the 
autoregressive integrated moving average (ARIMA) model. KSE -100, which 
represents the Stock Exchange of Pakistan. It plays a key role in reflecting the financial 
status of a country. Predicting stock market indices is important for investors. Policy 
decision makers and economists, because it is subject to inertial fluctuations and plays 
a role in economic planning. The study uses daily stock data from January 2012 to 
December 202 3, using the ARIMA model to predict future trends. Two models, 
ARIMA (1,0,1) and ARIMA (1,1,1), are evaluated and compared according to their 
performance. The results suggest that although the ARIMA (1,0,1) model is better 
suited to short -term forecasts, t he ARIMA (1,1,1) model captures the long -term 
uptrend more effectively. The study highlights the limitations of the ARIMA model in 
capturing the nonlinear nature of stock market movements. It suggests future 
exploration of hybrid models that combine tradit ional time series methods with 
machine learning approaches. These findings provide information on the effectiveness 
of the ARIMA model in financial forecasting. It has an impact on investors and 
policymakers who aim to reduce risk and optimize investment strategies. 
Keywords: Time series forecasting, Stock market prediction, ARIMA model, Pakistan 
Stock Exchange, Financial modeling

Introduction 
Time series prediction seeks to uncover the underlying relationships within 
time series data and analyze its evolving patterns based on historical observations. Due 
to extensive data sources across several domains, time series forecasting tasks have 
been widely implemented and have swiftly evolved into a crucial aspect of time series 
research. Theoretical studies and applications in several disciplines, including financial 
market analysis, traffic flow forecasting, human behavior prediction, and climate 
forecasting, define current research on time series prediction. Numerous predictive 
strategies have been developed during the past few decades from various modeling 
concepts and points of view (Jones, 2017; Kolambe & Arora, 2024; Zheng & Huang,

method. The ARIMA model integrates many techniques derived from the

Notwithstanding some limitations on parameter estimation and strict assumptions on 
the noise term, notable improvements in predictive accuracy have been obtained. Using 
its online optimization process, the ARIMA model efficiently estimates parameters, 
decreases training time, and increases its applicability to large -scale data, therefore 
addressing these difficulties (He et al., 2023; Ho & Xie, 1998; Khan & Alghulaiakh,

remains inadequately addressed.  In the presence of substantial noise, the variations 
between preceding and subsequent points in a time series may be markedly divergent 
and misleading. Nonstationary behavior significantly affects the precision of the 
ARIMA model. Differencing techniques struggle to reveal the original trend 
information amongst significant noise contamination. The influence of the online 
ARIMA may be more pronounced, and the extraction of sequential information is not 
sensitive in the one-pass training mode (Li et al., 2023). 
Research indicates that stock prices do not strictly adhere to the principles of 
random wandering or the efficient market hypothesis; rather, their price fluctuations 
exhibit a long-term memory effect in the stock market. The stock market is defined by 
significant risk and potential for substantial returns. Both securities institutions and 
investors exhibit a strong interest in the predictive analysis of the stock market, actively

predict future trends in stock prices by analyzing historical price movements. This

market, thereby enabling individuals to mitigate risks and enhance returns during stock

across multiple domains, including finance, climate science, and energy, among others.

as seasonal patterns, trends, a nd volatility. Several techniques, including 
Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) and Generalized Autoregressive 
Conditional Heteroskedasticity (GARCH), have been employed to predict univariate 
time series data. Nevertheless, these methods prim arily concentrate on linear trends, 
which may not consistently correspond to real-world data that frequently demonstrate

prices and stock trends remains a pressing challenge in the investment market. The

sentiment, which present significant challenges in accurately pre dicting stock 
movements (Song et al., 2023) . Over the past twenty years, the forecast for stock

prediction of stock returns. Although much literature has  emerged in nonlinear

The stock market represents the economic vitality of the actual industry . (Li, 
2023). The Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH) continues to be esteemed within the 
academic community. This hypothesis posits that stock prices fully incorporate all 
accessible information, while in inefficient stock markets, stock prices exhibit random 
fluctuations. Furthermore, the Random Walk Hypothesis posits that past share prices 
do not serve as a foundation for predicting future values. Consequently, share prices 
exhibit a random walk. Numerous investigations have been undertaken on the subject 
since the fo undational work referenced previously . (Ahmad et al., 2018) . Many 
statisticians and economists have argued historically about the random walk theory 
regarding stock market pricing. Advocates of the Random -walk hypothesis usually 
claim that the major stock exchanges across the world represent "efficient" markets. In 
an efficient market, the actions of many competing players produce actual prices that 
already integrate the consequences of current information, hence varying the price of 
a security from its inherent value. A market marked by independent sequential pric e 
swings in individual securities is, by definition, a random-walk market. According to 
the random walk hypothesis, existing stock prices reflect all the information at hand; 
therefore, a short period is required for new information to be incorporated into prices, 
which provides market participants with limited chances to profit from this knowledge. 
The homogeneity of information systems causes market players to act in a like manner, 
therefore reducing the above-average profit potential of new knowledge to the nominal 
average returns given by the market. Stock prices reflect all the relevant information 
that defines an efficient market. Consistent, unusual returns cannot be obtained from 
random walk prices.

The present research contribution to the area of financial forecasting is a 
conclusive appraisal of the effectiveness of ARIMA models to forecast the KSE -100 
Index. By comparing two configurations, ARIMA (1,0,1) and ARIMA (1,1,1), the 
research stresses their different strengths. The former permits good forecast precision 
in the immediate term, bolstered by an enhanced adjusted R-squared value (0.99892), 
so they can deal well with immediate market movements. On the other hand, ARIMA 
(1,1,1) fits long-term upward trends better, such that it agrees with the general growth 
path of the index. These empirical results accentuate the great value of determining the 
approaches needed concomitant with the horizon of the forecast. Also, the study 
enumerates drawbacks of ARIMA models, especially their inability to define non -
linearities and high volatility associated with financial markets. These findings echo 
previous studies th at indicated the same challenges faced while using ARIMA in 
volatile environments. (Zotteri et al., 2005)

Rationale 
The purpose of undertaking this research is to see how accurate ARIMA 
models are in predicting stock market indices, especially the KSE -100 Index of 
Pakistan (Afeef, Ali, & Khan, 2018; Marri & Talpur, 2021). The rationale for this is 
the need for efficient forecasting measures in a volatile and uncertain financial market. 
Predictions of the stock market indices are critical to investors, policymakers, and 
financial analysts as they will assist them in reducing risk and maximizing return on 
investments. Besides, the research studies the potential of applying ARIMA models for 
making precise short-term and long-term forecasts and increasing the pool of financial 
time series analysis literature. 
Problem Statement 
The instability, as well as the underlying unpredictory of financial markets, 
challenges the accurate forecast of stock indices (Li et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023). 
While ARIMA models are widely used in the prediction of financial data, the problem 
of the model’s limits for nonlinearity and nonstationarity remains. The study concerns 
the predictive capability of the ARIMA models for the KSE -100 index with two 
configurations. ARIMA (1,0,1) and ARIMA (1,1,1). The purpose of this research is to 
establish whether ARIMA models produce reliable forecasts for both short -term and 
long-term trends of the KSE-100 index and to determine the limitations and areas for 
improvement. 
Research Questions 
This part is constructed based on the available literature regarding the ARIMA 
models in financial forecasts (Bektemyssova et al., 2022; Mochurad & Dereviannyi, 
2024). 
1. How well do the ARIMA (1,0,1) and ARIMA (1,1,1) models model the short-term 
and long-run trends of the KSE-100 index? 
2. What are the strengths and weaknesses of ARIMA models in forecasting stock 
market indices through volatile circumstances? 
3. Can hybrid ARIMA models combined with a machine learning algorithm enhance 
the predictive accuracy of the KSE-100 index?

Theoretical Framework 
The theoretical basis of this study is rooted in time series analysis and , 
specifically, the Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) model (Box & 
Jenkins, 1976; Shumway & Stoffer, 2011). 
This study relies on the theoretical framework , which is based on time series 
analysis, and in its concentration, is applied to the Autoregressive Integrated Moving 
Average (ARIMA) model. The assumption behind the ARIMA model is that the 
previous observations of the time series and stochastic errors linearly determine the 
future observations for a certain time series . The study also largely depends on the 
Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH), along with the Random Walk Theory, which 
claims that the stock prices represent all the available information. As such, their prices 
cannot be predicted in efficient markets . The study aims to explore how machine 
learning methods can be incorporated into the process, such as Long Short -Term 
Memory (LSTM) networks, to avoid ARIMA’s disadvantages in detecting non-linear 
trends and volatility in the market. 
Conceptual Framework 
The conceptual framework entails the configuration of ARIMA models, as 
shown in previous studies (Afeef, Ihsan, & Zada, 2018; Liu, 2024). 
The conceptual framework involves a comparison of two constructs of an 
ARIMA model, the short -term ARIMA(1,0,1) and the long -term ARIMA (1,1,1), 
which are used for trend analysis. This is a study on how effective these models are in

techniques together to optimize predictive accuracy.

Literature Review 
Named for the Box-Jenkins method, the ARIMA model was first presented by 
George Box and Gwilym Jenkins in 1970. This method, which was first presented by 
Box and Jenkins in 1976, was further refined for time series forecasting in the 1990 s. 
According to Box and Jenkins' research from the 1970s, ARIMA is applied with less 
volatile data to create a systematic class of models for managing time -correlated 
modeling and forecasting (Shumway & Stoffer, 2011) . This model can be used to 
predict future values and define a time series using observed values . This method 
consists of a sequence of steps for identifying, estimating, and diagnosing ARIMA 
models based on time series data. When applied to any time series, these models reveal 
non-seasonal trends devoid of random white noise . To create an accurate stock 
forecasting model, the accuracy of an auto ARIMA model is compared to two 
customized ARIMA models. We need to be completely present right now and foresee 
future changes if we want to invest safely . Understanding the ARIMA model's 
applicability to time series forecasting and forecast accuracy is also beneficial . By 
contrasting an Auto ARIMA model with a bespoke ARIMA model, this study seeks to 
develop an accurate stock forecasting model . This emphasizes how time series 
forecasting methods need to be improved. In response to their use, econometric models 
have been upgraded. (Zotteri & colleagues, 2005).

The ARIMA model gives insights into economic indicator assessment, risk 
management techniques, stock price prediction, and so on. With informed investment 
choices, traders, investors, and financial analysts understand the complexities behind 
the financial markets and act on judgments that would be straightforward for them. Its 
content serves to analyze factors of credit risk, understand market dynamics, and build 
automatic trading plans. ARIMA remains the primary model, but one should know that 
it is a very large model with certain limits. Financial data are meant for an easy analysis 
with ARIMA, but this is not always the case when nonlinear components like human 
emotions and chance events are considered. Extreme events and structural changes 
could cause the model predictions to become dubious. Moreover, it may be that the 
choices regarding the parameters of the ARIMA model are, by definition, capricious 
and thus render uncertain. Besides, it may not be applicable to natural variables or for 
long-range forecasts. On the other hand, under these limitations, both scholars and 
financial analysts ought to act judiciously. There would be boosted accuracy and 
reliability of tests and forecasts when other methodologies and models are used. Even 
though aware that it remains a tool, acknowledging its limitations and adjusting it to 
fit financial data problems would go a long way in informing decision -making in the 
current dynamic financial environment. Bektemyssova et al. (2022) explain the usage

achieving control of non -stationary time series, which is in line with the traditional

but it has little application in volatile and non -linear data like Bitcoin. They advocate 
applying machine learning techniques, particularly LSTM, for accurate and reliable 
forecasting in cryptocurrency markets. 
Method  
The KSE -100 Index of the Pakistan Stock Exchange was selected as the 
sample stock index for the  study. Over twelve years and seven months, from January 
2012 to September 2024, secondary data were gathered from the Pakistan Stock 
Exchange, comprising daily values of the KSE-100 index. Trading occurs on five days 
per week. A total of 3,139 observations were employed in the investigation. The 
ARIMA model, Box and Jenkins (1976), is the most widely used time series approach. 
Empirical finance literature has primarily concentrated on forecasting returns and 
volatility, while it has overlooked the aspect of volume. The existing research on this 
topic is limited, with few contributions focused on volume forecasting. Certain studies 
employ neural networks and ARIMA models to predict monthly futures trading volume, 
highlighting the practical implications for the functioning of financial exchanges. Apart 
from its importance for estimating transaction costs and liquidity, volume forecasting 
is also amazing, as the time series traits of volatility and volume are so similar. Given 
the data of similar long-term dependency in both series, it is crucial to look at whether 
models with this quality can fairly project future volume and volatility (Lux & Kaizoji, 
2007). It is characterized by the premise that the future values of a variable are a linear

Discussions 
The study's conclusions offer a more comprehensive grasp of the financial 
market time series forecasting . Our analysis using ARIMA on the advantages and 
disadvantages of this statistical forecasting methodology on stock market movements, 
particularly that of the KSE -100, is reviewed in depth. The key conclusions of our 
empirical results also contradict previous works in the area. The main results of our 
analysis indicate that with a higher fitted R -squared value of 0.99892 and 0.01605 , 
ARIMA (1,0,1) provides a better fit than ARIMA (1,1,1). This implies that both were 
true. The forecasts by ARIMA (1,0,1) are preferred in terms of accuracy. This goes well 
with the past research performed by Afeef et al. (2018) related to the ARIMA model's 
robustness in stock market index prediction, especially concerning the short term. Our 
results of the ARIMA (1,0,1) forecast, however, show that ARIMA (1,0,1) has limited 
capability to mostly capture the overall upward trend of the KSE -100 Index, which 
seems evident. The ARIMA (1,1) model shows the best representation of the rising 
trend of the index. This result is similar to L i et al. (2017), who found that ARIMA 
models are unable to depict long -term trends in highly volatile markets. The ARIMA 
(1,0,1) model's incapacity to forecast significant changes in the future is derived from 
its linear structure. Non -linear deviations in duced by stock market data are ignored 
here. 
Different researchers have mentioned the forecasting challenges of financial 
time series data. On this issue, Zotteri et al. (2005) and Zotteri and Kalchschmidt (2007) 
argued that volatility has nothing to do with stock market indices and that ARIMA uses 
a rather subtle approach of time series modeling. This is reflected in our findings. The 
values of the sample deviation pattern of 12,880.47 and the Jarque -Bera statistic of 
359.52, which show a large departure from normality, confirm the acute volatility that 
we observe in the KSE -100 index. There are several examples of possibly more 
complex mode ls that take into account the nonlinearity and clustering features of 
volatility, such as the conditional autoregressive diversity (GARCH) model which was 
studied by Arashi and Rounaghi (2022), Bauwens et al. (2006), Lin (2018), and Shaik 
and Rehman (2023).

Conclusion 
Finally, this study demonstrates the performance of the ARIMA model on the 
KSE-100 index, where the ARIMA (1,0,1) model provides superior adaptation to short-
term shocks and the ARIMA (1,1,1) model catches the long-term trend and remains a 
valuable reference for time series forecasting. Limitations in capturing non -linear 
movements and market fluctuations must be acknowledged. Future research should 
explore the integration of machine learning techniques and macroeconomic variables. 
To increase the accuracy and robustness of both prediction models. The findings of this 
study have serious implications for investors and decision -makers, focusing on the 
need for timely forecasts. For those investors who would like to predict market 
movement, the ability of the ARIMA (1,1,1) model to detect uptrends in the KSE-100 
Index provides valuable insights. Forecasts of  ARIMA (1,1,1), although appearing to 
be a good fit for short-term periods, appear to be more accurate in predicting medium-
term behavior that conforms to long -term trends. The findings indicate that 
understanding market volatility and the limitations of conventional forecasting models 
are essential for policymakers. In their articles, Che et al. (2024), Kehinde et al. (2023), 
and Xu and Zhang (2023) insisted that precise forecasting models may be of use to 
traders and policymakers alike  in making informed decisions regarding market 
intervention and regulation. High volatility on the KSE -100 index suggests that 
authorities should closely monitor market movements and prepare for possible 
interventions during severe instabilities. 
With the reserves of this study, we certainly have given good bases in terms of 
the ARIMA model application in the KSE -100 index. First, regarding the robustness 
of this work, the straight -line aspect of these models limits it to the ARIMA models, 
thus forsaking the very complexity of stock markets. The very high volatility of the 
KSE-100 index and its anomalies may imply the applicability of some other models , 
such as GARCH or even LSTM networks (Mualifah et al., 2024; García -Medina & 
Aguayo-Moreno, 2024) , to increase forecast accuracy, particularly in long -term 
patterns (Marri & Talpur, 2021; Afeef et al., 2020). Second, the historical data of the 
KSE-100 index were solely utilized in this study, which may have restricted the 
generalizability of the findings in the stock market. Future studies can then expand 
upon this investigation with the addition of other models and comparisons of ARIMA 
with different indices to see if any similar trend appears. Additionally, external factors 
such as interest  rates and macroeconomic variables like inflation may impede the 
forecasting power of the model (Liyanage, 2023). Another limitation of the ARIMA 
model may be addressed regarding its adjustments for non -linear oscillations within 
the financial markets. Fut ure studies should consider the possible synergies between 
these utilized machine learning approaches and classical time  series modeling. A 
combination of GARCH or ARIMA with an LSTM might provide a comprehensive 
prediction structure. To increase efficiency and clarity, these datasets will be calculated

Recommendations 
1. Machine learning techniques would typically be LSTM and GARCH modeling 
(Mualifah, Soleh, & Notodiputro, 2024) to compensate for ARIMA's downside and 
extract nonlinear trends from stock market data. 
2. Expand the study to include more stock indices and macroeconomic variables to 
have a more solid ground for predicting analysis (Li et al., 2023). 
3. Develop hybrid models that will integrate advanced machine learning techniques 
with statistical time series methods to enhance prediction accuracy in the 
unpredictable world of finance (Nasir et al., 2023). 
4. To make up for ARIMA's failure to detect the nonlinear patterns in the stock market 
data, combine different machine learning techniques, such as LSTM and GARCH 
models.

variables for a more robust forecasting analysis. 
6. Create hybrid models that will integrate statistical time series with state-of-the-art

Limitations 
1. The study is focused only on the KSE -100 index and does not relate to any other 
stock indexes or financial markets, as per Ahmad et al. (2018).  
2. The major disadvantage of the ARIMA model is its linear assumptions, which are 
not adequate to take into account the nonlinearity of stock market data (Mochurad 
& Dereviannyi, 2024).  
3. The study is based solely on past information, which will hamper the model's 
capability to foresee new market shocks or black swans (Singh et al., 2024).  
4. The KSE-100 index restricted the study's findings, and these findings may not 
apply to other stock indices or even other financial markets.
